# Hackathon Pitch & Presentation Guide: ShaderMind

> [!NOTE]
> **The Core Lieberman Pitch (The Hook):**
> *"We didn't build a machine to replace the artist. We built a machine to sustain the artist’s most difficult task: showing up every day. This is a system that learns your aesthetic, holds your history, and ensures that even on the days you have only five minutes, your creative practice never stops."*

---

## Slide 1: The Problem — The Friction of "Showing Up"
* **The Frame:** The hardest part of creative work isn't writing the code; it’s showing up to the blank page every single day. Zach Lieberman sketched every day for 10 years (3,650 days). But most of us quit because of creative friction, self-doubt, and the weight of perfectionism.
* **The AI Landscape:** Current AI tools turn humans into passive, lazy prompt-to-image consumers. We don’t need more generators; we need a system that supports, nudges, and sustains our creative practice.

---

## Slide 2: The Solution — ShaderMind
* **What it is:** An autonomous, self-improving creative coding partner that helps users build a daily sketching habit. It generates live-running, interactive WebGL shader canvases (GLSL) in real-time.
* **The Core Philosophy:** Built directly on Zach Lieberman's three tenets of daily sketching:
  1. **Rhythm over Perfection:** Shaders are generated in fast, low-stakes batches of 10.
  2. **Remixing History:** *"Make something new out of something old."* Code is never written from a blank slate; it inherits and mutates from yesterday's successes.
  3. **Visual Diary:** The gallery is a **"Studio Journal"** chronicling the raw journey—failures, compile glitches, and favorite breakthroughs alike.

---

## Slide 3: The "Lieberman" Differentiators
* **The 5-3-2 Evolutionary Rule Engine:**
  * **5 Evolutionary Shaders:** Inherits and mutates previous "Good" sketch parameters.
  * **3 Directive Shaders:** Aligns with the user's latest aesthetic guidelines.
  * **2 Mutation Shaders:** Random mathematical injections (e.g. Raymarching, Voronoi, 3D Noise) to break creative ruts.
* **DJ Does Not Take Requests (Stubborn AI Voice):** The agent isn't a subservient chat bot. It has its own artistic manifesto. When you give it feedback, it interprets it through its stubborn artistic lens rather than executing a mindless search-and-replace.
* **The K.G. (Keep Going) Metric:** Shows a "Chain of Days" and streak count. The primary metric isn't "art," it's "consistency."

---

## Slide 4: Continual Learning Demonstration
*This is the slide to visually prove the Continual Learning theme to technical judges:*
* **The Self-Correction Loop:** When a user rates a batch, the model reflects, logs its self-criticism in a public terminal, and updates its strategy guide.
* **Real-time Evolution:** The prompt instructions are updated in a database timeline, showing a direct genetic line of descent from Gen 0 to Gen 100 based entirely on user curation.

---

## 🎬 The Winning 60-Second Demo Script

1. **0:00 - 0:15 | The System Hook**
   * *Visual:* Dark glassmorphic interface on screen.
   * *Script:* "This is ShaderMind. We didn't build an AI to replace artists; we built an AI to help us show up. Our goal is 10 years of daily sketches—3,650 items. Here is our Keep Going milestone tracker."
2. **0:15 - 0:35 | Human Curation**
   * *Visual:* Click "Generate Batch." 10 glowing interactive canvases compile and render in seconds.
   * *Action:* Mark 3 canvases as "Good". In the textbox, type: *"Too fast and chaotic. Make the motion slower, more liquid, like an exposed candle flame."*
3. **0:35 - 0:50 | The Self-Reflection Loop**
   * *Visual:* Click "Submit & Evolve." Zoom into the **Agent Self-Reflection Log** on the screen.
   * *Script:* "Our agent doesn't just copy-paste. It reflects, writes an internal critique, and mutates its strategy."
   * *Console Stream on Screen:* *"Analyzing Gen #4. User rejected rapid grid movements but favored organic curves. Discarding sterile grid mathematics. Integrating a slow liquid damping factor to coordinate mouse movement, simulating a delicate flame in the wind."*
4. **0:50 - 1:00 | Real-Time Proof**
   * *Visual:* The next batch of 10 renders. The movement is instantly slower, fluid, and incorporates the evolved math.
   * *Script:* "In real-time, the agent has evolved its visual engine. Our creative practice never stops."
